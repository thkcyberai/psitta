Plain Text in Psitta

Sometimes you just need a blank space and your thoughts. No fonts, no formatting, no decisions — just words. That's what a plain text file (.txt) is for: capturing an idea before it slips away.

In Psitta, plain text is a full writing format. Jot a note, draft a scene, list your ideas, brain-dump a chapter. You can edit it freely in the Writing Desk, and you can press Read to hear it spoken back — even a rough draft sounds clearer out loud.

Plain text is also the most durable format there is. A .txt file written today will open, unchanged, in fifty years. There is something freeing about that: nothing to break, nothing to update, just your words.

Use plain text when you want zero friction. When the draft grows up and needs headings and polish, move it into a Word document or export it. Until then, this is your napkin and your pen.

Write something here. Then listen to it. That's the whole idea.
